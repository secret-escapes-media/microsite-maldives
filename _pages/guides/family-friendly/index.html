--- layout: guide group: explore group-order: 3 id: family-friendly title: Family-friendly Maldives intro-short: It’s not just for honeymooners - the heavenly islands are safe and fun-filled playgrounds for kids, too. intro: Once seen as an adults-only zone for honeymooners and couples, the Maldives is becoming increasingly popular with families for its incredibly safe environs and activity-led resorts. Choose your island carefully and you can enjoy the very best of barefoot luxury, while the little ones have a ball. As a guide, here are some considerations to help you pick the right paradise for your pack. sections: - title: Villas content: >

Beach villas are probably the best choice if you’re travelling with a baby or very young children. Aside from the obvious safety qualms, they tend to offer the most space and are more conveniently located for accessing the resort facilities.

- title: Activities content: >

Some resorts are more activity-focused than others. It’s nice to think that you might sit around as a family playing rummy until the sun sets, but for when you need to pull out the big guns, watersports and child-friendly excursions are key. Plus, gazing out from an infinity pool is fine, but the view from a fun tube? Something else.

small-sections: - title: Services content: >

A kids club is a must. Not only will it keep them entertained, it will give you a chance to disappear for a Maldivian spa treatment while they play. Most resorts offer a babysitter on request. It’s also worth checking if there’s an on-island doctor, a kids pool and children’s menus.

- title: Transport content: >

Hats off to those who’ll accept the challenge of a domestic flight after touching down in Malé. If reaching your destination as quickly and seamlessly as possible is what you have in mind, consider a resort that’s not too far from the mainland. Speedboat transfers are quicker, more affordable and convenient if you’re landing outside of daylight hours.

- title: Conservation content: >

When you’re visiting somewhere as pristine as the Maldives, it’s a joy to help the resort staff with their conservation efforts. Children can often participate in projects including coral regeneration - the biggest challenge for the archipelago. A wonderful way for the kids to earn their keep.
